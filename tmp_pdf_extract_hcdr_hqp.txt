Abstract A redundant Hybrid Cable-Driven Robot
(HCDR), which is an integration platform of a robotic
manipulator and a Cable-Driven Parallel Robot (CDPR)
has numerous advantages including an extensive oper-
ational range and high performance in task execu-
tion. However, it is difﬁcult to control the redun-
dant HCDR due to the dynamic coupling between a
manipulator and the CDPR and the redundancy res-
olution. To solve these issues, this paper introduces
a novel whole-body controller strategy for the redun-
dant HCDR by using Hierarchical Quadratic Program-
ming (HQP). First, the whole-body nonlinear dynamics
with cable tensions and manipulator torques is pro-
posed. Then, with the HQP-based scheme, the pro-
posed controller can generate the optimal tensions and
torques for the redundant HCDR to generate whole-
body motions without dynamic interference between
the CDPR and the manipulator. In addition, singular-
ity and joint limit avoidance algorithms are proposed
in this paper, because the proposed controller can han-
dle prioritized tasks with inequality constraints as well
as equality constraints. The proposed control scheme
has been implemented on the redundant HCDR with 8-

1 Introduction
A Cable-Driven Parallel Robot (CDPR) is one of the
unique parallel robot mechanisms that utilize cables
to manipulate and control the motion of the platform
[20,33]. The CDPR not only enables precise control of
motion but also handles heavy objects by adjusting the
length and tension of the cables [ 35]. With these advan-
tages, the CDPR is highly adaptable to various environ-
ments such as construction [ 12], aerospace [ 38], med-
ical [ 11], and entertainment ﬁelds [ 23].
Recently, many studies have been conducted to max-
imize the advantages of the CDPR by combining it with
other mechanical devices such as a mobile robot [ 15],
gripper [36], a pendulum [ 31], and a robotic manipula-
tor [ 19,32]. Among them, a combination of the CDPR
with the manipulator, known as the redundant Hybrid
Cable-Driven Robot (HCDR), can not only extend the
workspace of the robotic arm but also enhance the dex-
terity and ﬂexibility of the CDPR.
However, there are main challenging issues in con-
trolling the redundant HCDR, as follows:

1.1 Related works
1.1.1 Cable-driven parallel robot
There are several researches for controlling the CDPR
from a traditional kinematic controller to a Nonlinear
Model Predictive Control (NMPC). Unlike controllers
for fully actuated robotic manipulators, controllers for
CDPRs should focus on generating proper tensions of
cables to prevent cable wear and provide precise posi-
tioning tasks [ 1].
Mersi et al. proposed a kinematics-based PID con-
troller for the CDPR with four cables to generate cable
tension [ 21]. Pott and Schmidt suggested the inverse
kinematics controller to minimize the potential ener-
gies in the cables [ 29]. However, these controllers lack
consideration for the characteristics of cables which
have elastic deformation, geometric nonlinearity, and
friction [27]. Especially, in case of the CDPR, the con-
troller should include constraints on the cable tension
because the cables of the CDPR can only be pulled, not
pushed.
To address this issue, many control methods have
been proposed including a sliding mode control [ 40],
a fuzzy plus PI control [ 39], a Laypunov-based con-
trol [ 24], and a robust PID controller [ 14] with the
optimal distribution of cable tensions [ 10]. As a very
recent work, Piao et al. proposed an adaptive hybrid
control approach with Cartesian space position control
and a joint space cable force control [ 28]. The pro-
posed controller has the advantage of improving the
position control accuracy by minimizing the cable ten-
sion to prevent sagging or overloading of the CDPR.
Also, Santos et al. proposed an NMPC strategy incorpo-
rating wrench equivalent optimality for position track-
ing of the CDPR [ 34]. However, the proposed NMPC
approach, despite its potential, faces signiﬁcant chal-
lenges in real-time implementation due to the high com-
putational cost associate with solving nonlinear opti-
mization problems for the nonlinear dynamics of the
CDPR.
1.1.2 Redundant hybrid cable-driven robot
While the aforementioned controllers are capable of
effectively controlling the CDPR system, applying
them directly to the redundant HCDR is highly chal-
lenging due to the dynamic coupling between a manip-
ulator and the CDPR and a redundancy of a whole sys-
tem [ 3,30].
In [ 2], the controller for a 2-DoFs planar manipu-
lator with the CDPR is proposed to enhance its capa-
bility to achieve a wider range of orientations along a
given trajectory. Gouttefarde studied the static behav-
ior of the entire system consisting of the CDPR with
2-cables and the on-board 3-DoFs planar manipulator
[9]. Similarly, Lessanibahri et al. analyzed the redun-
dant HCDR [ 19] with the CDPR platform and tilt-roll
wrist for large translational and rotational workspace.
Although these researches demonstrated the high per-

ious simulation scenarios. However, these researches
are difﬁcult to extend the versatility of the controller
for tasks such as joint limits or singularity avoid-
ance, because the proposed controllers can only handle
equality constraints not inequality constraints.
Consequently, to the best of our knowledge, there is
no proper controller for redundant HCDRs that consid-
ers whole-body motion, nonlinear dynamic coupling,
and robotic constraints during operation. This is the
biggest motivation for this paper.
1.2 Overview of this paper
In this paper, we propose a novel controller for generat-
ing whole-body motions of the redundant HCDR with
nonlinear whole-body dynamics and the HQP in order
to maximize the stability and accuracy of the redun-
dant HCDR. The HQP is an optimization technique to
handle multi-level hierarchical equality and inequality
tasks. Recent studies have shown that HQP-based con-
trol can achieve computational speeds comparable to
traditional Jacobian-based control, making it suitable
for real-time applications in complex robotic systems.
Moreover, the HQP-based controller is stable in the
sense of Lyapunov [ 6]. Therefore, various studies have
utilized HQP not only to control robotic arms [ 16]b u t
also a quadruped robot [ 37] and a mobile manipulator
[17].
The main contributions in this paper are listed as
follows:
• A modeling of the nonlinear whole-body dynam-
ics of the redundant HCDR is proposed. This pro-
posed modeling describes the dynamic coupling
between the CDPR and the robotic arm, effec-
tively preventing instabilities that arise from their
interaction. Through experimental validation, we
demonstrated that by compensating for the nonlin-
ear forces induced by dynamic coupling, our mod-
eling signiﬁcantly enhances both the stability and
tracking performance of the redundant HCDR con-
trol system.
• With various advantages of the HQP , the proposed
controller can handle the complex redundancy res-
olution of the HCDR. Unlike the Jacobian-based
controller [ 4,22], our approach can handle both
equality and inequality constraints simultaneously,
enabling precise control of cable tensions within
safe operational limits, and generating optimal, fea-
sible whole-body motions that fully exploit the
redundancy of the redundant HCDR.
• For real-world applications, various practical con-
trol techniques are developed and implemented.
Our approach ensures smooth operation through
singularity avoidance even near kinematic singu-
larities, maintains safe joint conﬁgurations through
joint limit avoidance, and achieves real-time per-
formance with control loop times.
The proposed algorithm is validated in the a high-
precision robot simulator, Gazebo [18], by tracking
capability and stability during various scenarios.

ical backgrounds of CDPR and HQP are explained in
Sect. 2. Section 3 presents the HQP-based whole-body
controller with the nonlinear dynamics of the redun-
dant HCDR and Sect. 4 describes practical applications
including singularity and joint limit avoidance. Sec-
tion 5 describes the experimental results and discussion
of the proposed controller and the paper is concluded
in Sect. 6.

4.1 Singularity avoidance
Kinematic singularity refers to a situation where the
manipulator loses one or more DoFs, resulting in unsta-
ble behavior. Although the damped pseudo inverse
is the well-known method to avoid the singularity,
it is not directly applicable to the HQP formulation.
Thus, in this subsection, similar to our previous work
with QR decomposition [ 16], the singularity avoid-
ance method using the Singular V alue Decomposition
(SVD) is applied to the proposed control strategy.
This method is based on the idea that when a sin-
gularity occurs, the robot cannot move in a particu-
lar direction. Generally speaking, although singularity
issues do not arise because of its redundancy of the
HCDR, it may sometimes produce excessive stretch-
ing motion. Therefore, considering SVD of the Jaco-
bian for the manipulator can be an effective approach
in these situations.
The main idea is that motion in the directions where
the manipulator cannot move is achieved through the
platform. Mathematically, the directions where the
manipulator lacks mobility are represented by the SVD
of the Jacobian. For the full-rank Jacobian, J

5.3 Further discussion
5.3.1 Transferring simulation to real
In this paper, while the controller is validated only in
a simulation environment, there may be some practi-
cal issues in real-world experiments that could degrade
the control performance of the proposed framework.

cables, and low control frequency can signiﬁcantly

the control input to prevent the system from accumu-

6 Conclusion
The redundant HCDR with a robot manipulator has
great advantages in various ﬁelds, including con-
struction, aerospace, and entertainment. However, the
dynamic coupling between the CDPR platform and
a robot arm causes the instability of the redundant
HCDR. In this paper, a novel whole-body controller
for the redundant HCDR with HQP-based controller is
proposed. The whole-body nonlinear dynamics mod-
eling is discussed to avoid not only the inaccuracy,
but also the instability of the motions of the redun-
dant HCDR while minimizing the tension of the cable.
Especially, through the advantages of the HQP-based
controller, the proposed algorithm can handle inequal-
ity constraints as well as equality constraints. Based
on the proposed controller, various techniques to avoid
singularity and joint limit are introduced in this paper.
We have demonstrated the performance of the pro-
posed controller through several experiments using a
highly accurate physics-based simulator. Our future
work will involve developing a position and orienta-
tion estimation algorithm and implementing the actual
HCDR platform with the proposed controller.
